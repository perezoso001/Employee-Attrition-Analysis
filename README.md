## üß≠ Project Overview
This project focuses on **analyzing employee attrition data** within an organization using **Microsoft Excel**.  
The goal is to understand the **factors driving employee turnover**, identify **patterns across demographics and departments**, and provide **actionable insights** that HR and management can use to improve **employee retention**.

---

## üéØ Project Objectives
- Determine the **overall attrition rate** and its variations across job roles, departments, and demographics.  
- Understand how **factors like tenure, satisfaction, and performance** influence attrition.  
- Identify **key demographics and performance groups** with high attrition.  
- Provide **data-driven recommendations** to enhance employee engagement and retention.

## üìò Final Analysis Summary

| Area | Key Insights |
|------|---------------|
| **Job & Organizational Factors** | Highest attrition in **sales**, **technical roles**, and **early tenure** employees. Lower among those with **advanced degrees** or **longer service**. |
| **Demographic Factors** | **Younger, single employees** more likely to leave. Some trends indicate higher attrition among **women**, influenced by **age, marital status, and gender**. |
| **Work Experience & Satisfaction** | Employees with **low job satisfaction**, **frequent overtime**, or **low performance** are more prone to leave. Even **high performers** may leave due to lack of **recognition** or **growth opportunities**. |
| **Consolidated Actions** | 1. Regularly check in with disengaged employees.<br>2. Act on employee feedback.<br>3. Support or reward those working overtime.<br>4. Offer **growth and learning opportunities** to younger staff. |
